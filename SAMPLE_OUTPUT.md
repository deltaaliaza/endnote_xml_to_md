## Abstract

The study uses technology acceptance model (TAM) to gain insights into user reactions to the technology adoption of Zoom application in language course. A sample of 227 students of a public university in Saudi Arabia who were exposed to language instruction through Zoom participated in this quantitative study. The results of Partial Least Squares Structural Equation Modeling (PLS-SEM) showed that perceived ease of use and perceived usefulness are significant predictors of the attitude towards Zoom, also the attitude is significant predictor of behavioral intention. In addition to the direct relationships, the perceived ease of use has an indirect effect on behavioral intention through perceived usefulness, suggesting that learner can develop intention to use Zoom if they find it easy to use and useful for language learning. Theoretical and practical implications are discussed in the paper.
